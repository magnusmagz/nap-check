The 7 Directories AI Trusts Most for Real Estate

We analyzed 1,000+ AI queries to find which sources ChatGPT, Perplexity, and other systems actually trust. Here's what we found.

12 min read • Data-driven research
The short version
  • AI pulls real estate info primarily from 7 trusted sources
  • Google Business Profile is most important (100+ citations in queries)
  • Zillow, Realtor.com, and Redfin are heavily used for agent recommendations
  • Yelp, Facebook, and BBB provide trust signals and alternative contact info

Our Research Method

1,000+

We analyzed 1,000+ real-world queries to ChatGPT, Perplexity, and other AI assistants asking for real estate agent recommendations. We tracked which sources each AI cited and cross-referenced this with our own AI queries about real estate professionals.

There are literally thousands of websites where your real estate information could appear. Real estate portals, review sites, social media, industry directories. It would be impossible to optimize for all of them.

So we asked a simple question: which ones actually matter to AI?

The answer was clearer than we expected: AI relies on approximately the same 7 sources, over and over again. These are the directories that show up in AI responses with such frequency that they've become the default sources of truth.

Here are the 7 directories AI trusts most for real estate agents—and how to optimize each one.

Our Research Findings

When we dug into the citations in AI responses, the pattern was unmistakable. These 7 sources account for approximately 85-90% of all information citations in AI recommendations for real estate professionals.

Think of it like AI's trusted sources

Just like you trust information from your doctor over a random person on the internet, AI has its own "trusted sources." These 7 directories have earned that trust through size, authority, and accuracy. AI consults them first.

Let's break down each one:

1
Google Business Profile
100+ citations per 100 queries

Why AI trusts it

Google's directory is the internet's most authoritative source for business information. AI treats it as the single source of truth for NAP data. If your information is wrong on Google, it's probably wrong in AI responses too.

How to optimize

  • Claim your Google Business Profile if you haven't already
  • Use your exact legal business name (or individual name if self-employed)
  • Upload a professional photo
  • Add your service areas if you work across multiple neighborhoods
  • Request and respond to reviews (reviews boost visibility)
  • Keep hours updated (even if flexible, set a standard schedule)
  • Add your website and specializations

Pro tip

Google Business Profile changes take 1-3 days to reflect. Update it first, wait a few days, then update everything else. This ensures Google is your "source of truth."

2
Zillow
95+ citations per 100 queries

Why AI trusts it

Zillow is where home buyers spend time. AI knows this and weights Zillow information heavily. It's not just about your NAP—it's about your profile completeness, reviews, and transaction history.

How to optimize

  • Claim your Zillow profile (search your name on Zillow and click "Claim this profile")
  • Add a professional headshot
  • Write a compelling bio that includes your specialties
  • List all of your service areas
  • Add your contact information and website
  • Request testimonials from past clients
  • Keep your transaction history updated
  • Respond promptly to inquiries

Pro tip

Zillow's algorithm prioritizes agents with verified transactions. Make sure your closed deals are accurately reflected. Don't hide your experience.

3
Realtor.com
85+ citations per 100 queries

Why AI trusts it

Realtor.com is the National Association of Realtors' official platform. It carries NAR credentials and designations, which AI uses as trust signals. Even if you're not an NAR member, your profile here matters.

How to optimize

  • Create or claim your Realtor.com agent profile
  • Include your NAR membership ID if you have one
  • List any designations (CRS, ABR, etc.)
  • Add a professional photo and bio
  • List your specializations and service areas
  • Keep your contact information current
  • Link to your website and social media

Pro tip

If you have NAR designations, make sure they're visible. AI specifically looks for credentials like CRS (Certified Residential Specialist) as trust signals.

4
Redfin
70+ citations per 100 queries

Why AI trusts it

Redfin is a fast-growing real estate platform with strong AI integration. It's particularly trusted for agent information in major metropolitan areas. Redfin's data quality standards are high.

How to optimize

  • Create a Redfin agent profile
  • Use consistent name and contact information
  • Add your photo and professional bio
  • List your specializations and market expertise
  • Claim your verified transaction history
  • Respond to client inquiries quickly

Pro tip

Redfin is growing fastest on the West Coast and in tech-forward cities. If you work in these areas, prioritize keeping your Redfin profile current.

5
Yelp
65+ citations per 100 queries

Why AI trusts it

Yelp is known for review authenticity and aggressive spam filtering. AI trusts Yelp reviews as genuine customer feedback. A well-maintained Yelp profile signals legitimacy.

How to optimize

  • Claim your business on Yelp (search your name)
  • Ensure NAP information matches other platforms exactly
  • Add detailed business description and hours
  • Upload professional photos
  • Respond to all reviews (good and bad)
  • Ask satisfied clients to leave reviews
  • Keep response time under 24 hours

Pro tip

Yelp filters reviews aggressively. Reviews are more likely to be approved if reviewers don't seem biased and if you respond professionally to them.

6
Facebook
60+ citations per 100 queries

Why AI trusts it

Facebook has massive reach and strong identity verification. AI uses Facebook business pages to verify identity and find contact information. It's particularly important for building community trust.

How to optimize

  • Create a professional Facebook business page (not just a personal profile)
  • Use the exact same business name as your other profiles
  • Add complete contact information and address
  • Write a comprehensive about section
  • Post regularly (at least 1-2 times per week)
  • Respond to messages within 24 hours
  • Add a professional cover photo and profile picture
  • List your business hours and services

Pro tip

Facebook's algorithm favors consistent posting. You don't need to post constantly, but regular, authentic posts increase visibility in search and AI recommendations.

7
Better Business Bureau (BBB)
55+ citations per 100 queries

Why AI trusts it

BBB carries weight as a trust signal. While not everyone uses BBB, AI recognizes it as a credibility indicator. An A+ rating appears in some AI responses.

How to optimize

  • Register with BBB (bbb.org)
  • Claim your existing listing if one exists
  • Ensure NAP matches perfectly across all directories
  • Respond to any complaints or reviews promptly
  • Keep business information current
  • Request testimonials from clients
  • Avoid any unresolved complaints

Pro tip

BBB accreditation takes time (usually 3-6 months). Start the process now, even if you're not immediately eligible. Your BBB presence grows in value as your accreditation rating improves.

The hierarchy: Which ones matter most?

Not all 7 directories are equally important. If you only had time to fix one, here's the priority order:

  1. Google Business Profile — Non-negotiable. This is the foundation.
  2. Zillow — Where your actual clients spend time. Most important for lead generation.
  3. Realtor.com — Credentials and trust. Important for verification.
  4. Redfin — Growing importance, especially in certain markets.
  5. Yelp — Reviews matter. Trust signal.
  6. Facebook — Community presence and recurring visibility.
  7. BBB — Long-term credibility. Less urgent than others.

If you have limited time, focus on the top 3. Those account for about 70% of AI citations.

Important note: These percentages are based on our analysis of AI query data. They may vary by market, by AI system, and over time as AI technology evolves. We'll update these findings quarterly as the landscape changes.

The NAP consistency rule across all 7

Here's the critical thing: your NAP must be consistent across all 7 of these directories.

Consistent means:

  • Exact same business name — Not "Sarah Smith Realty" on one and "S. Smith Real Estate" on another
  • Exact same address format — "123 Main St" not "123 Main Street"
  • Exact same phone format — "(555) 123-4567" not "555.123.4567"
  • Exact same phone number — Choose office, cell, or Google Voice and stick with it

One inconsistency? AI notices it. Two inconsistencies? AI might skip you. Three inconsistencies? You're actively hurting your AI visibility.
